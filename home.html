      Sanmenxia, a prefecture-level city under the jurisdiction of Henan Province, is located in the Golden Triangle area of the Yellow River in the south of Henan, Shanxi and Shaanxi. The landform is dominated by mountains, hills and loess plateaus. 2 districts, 2 counties, and 2 county-level cities hosted by the city, with a permanent population of 2.276 million in 2019.
History

      According to legend, Dayu harnessed the water and used his axe to split the mountain into three gorges: "Human Gate", "Shen Gate", and "Ghost Gate", which led the water of the Yellow River to the east. The jurisdiction is located in the foothills of the Qinling Yumai Mountain, and stands in the territory of the ancient valley of the Hangu Pass. Sanmenxia is also known as "Xiahan" because it was called "Xiahan" in the ancient times. Six thousand years ago, a larger clan appeared. Around the 21st century to the 13th century, this was the central area ruled by the Xia and Shang dynasties. During the Western Zhou Dynasty, it was Jiao and Guo. Spring and Autumn first belong to Guo and then Jin. During the Warring States period they belonged to Han, Qin and Wei. Qin belongs to Sanchuan County. Han is the Hongnong County. The Three Kingdoms belong to the Hengnong County of Wei. Jin is located in Hongnong County, Sizhou. The Northern Wei Dynasty continued until the Ming and Qing Dynasties after it was set up in Shaanzhou. During the period of the Republic of China, it first belonged to West Henan Road and Heluo Road, and was later changed to the 11th Administrative Inspectorate District of Henan Province. Before and after the founding of the People's Republic of China, the Shaanzhou Commissioner's Office was established. In 1952, the Shaanzhou and Luoyang offices were merged. In March 1957, the State Council approved the establishment of Sanmenxia City, under the direct jurisdiction of Henan Province. In 1961, Sanmenxia City was reduced from a prefecture-level city to a county-level city. In 1986, with the approval of the State Council, Sanmenxia City was again upgraded to a prefecture-level city. On March 6, 2015, in accordance with the “Reply of the State Council on Approving Henan Province to Adjust Some Administrative Divisions in Sanmenxia City”, some administrative divisions in Sanmenxia City were adjusted, Shaanxian County was abolished, and Shaanzhou District in Sanmenxia City was established. The administrative area of ​​the county is the administrative area of ​​Shaanzhou District. The Shaanzhou District Government is located at No. 18, Shaanzhou Avenue, Daying Town. Culture
      Yangshao culture, Taoism culture and Guo culture all originated in Sanmenxia. They are also the founding ground of the Chinese emperor Huangdi, the writing site of Lao Tzu's Tao Te Ching, and the dead place of Buddhist ancestor Bodhidharma. There are 6 key cultural relics protection units nationwide, 33 provincial cultural relics protection units, and more than 70,000 cultural relics in the collection. The Lingbao Xipo site was added as one of the top six sites for Chinese civilization source exploration projects in the country. The ancient trench section of Weihan Ancient Road was selected as a world cultural heritage site. The Yangshao Village Cultural Site, Miaodigou Cultural Site, Shangyang City and The three excavations of the Guoguo cemetery were selected as one of China's top 100 archaeological discoveries in the 20th century. Yangshao Culture In 1921, Yangshao Culture, named after Yangshao Village in Mianchi County, was first discovered in the late Neolithic period, 5000 to 7000 years ago, distributed in the middle and lower reaches of the Yellow River. The painted pottery on the surface is one of its important features. . A total of more than 5,000 cultural relics in Yangshao have been discovered nationwide, including more than 200 in Sanmenxia City. The Yangshao Village site carried out three excavation studies in October 1921, June 1951, October-November 1980, and March-April 1981. The discovery of Yangshao culture uncovered the first page of Chinese field archaeology, the first page of Chinese neolithic archaeology, the first page of Chinese archaeological research, and the first page of Chinese primitive society research, which became an epoch-making milestone. One of "100 Great Archaeological Discoveries in China in the 20th Century". Yellow Emperor Culture The Huangdi Zhuding original settlement site group is located in Yangping Town, Lingbao City. It is surrounded by the Qinling Mountains in the south, the Yellow River in the north, the Shahe River in the east, and the Yangping River in the west. There are 50 cultural relics from the Yangshao period within 300 square kilometers, with a total area of 4.36 square kilometer. "Historical Records", "Hanshu", "Zizhitongjian" and other documents all have records of the Yellow Emperor's "Ding Ding Sheng Tian". There are also legends related to the Yellow Emperor such as the Yellow Emperor Zhuding, Yulong Ascension, and the Ancestor Caisang around Zhuding, as well as many mountain, water, and place names related to the Yellow Emperor, especially Xuanyuantai and Kuafu. The phenomenon of Shan and Chiyou Mountain named after the tribal leaders at that time was rare in the country. The Huangdi Zhuding original settlement site group and the records and legends about the Yellow Emperor confirmed each other, which is the best starting point for the Chinese civilization exploration project (the Five Emperors Project). Among them, the Lingbao Xipo site was included in the six preferred sites for the Chinese civilization source exploration project, and the Beiyangping site group was included in the 100 key major site protection projects by the National Development and Reform Commission and the State Administration of Cultural Heritage. In 2006, the Xipo site was selected as one of the top ten new national archaeological discoveries of the year. Guo culture Guo Guo is a princely kingdom with the surname of Ji established in the early period of the Western Zhou Dynasty. It has a wide and profound influence on the history and culture of the Western Zhou Dynasty. In the 1950s, the National Yellow River Reservoir Archaeological Team discovered the Guoguo cemetery in Shangcunling, Sanmenxia City, and excavated it for the first time. In the 1990s, the Henan Provincial Institute of Cultural Relics and Archaeology and the Sanmenxia City Archaeological Team jointly conducted the second large-scale excavation of the graveyard of Guo State. Among them, the M2001 Guo Ji tomb excavated in 1990 and the M2009 Guo Zhong tomb excavated in 1991 were rated as one of the “Top Ten New Archaeological Discoveries in China”. The large number of cultural relics unearthed twice, the complete variety, the fine production, and the high value are rare in archaeology in the two weeks since the founding of New China. In 1984, the 1727 Chema Hang, which was preserved for the first time, was excavated for the first time to build the Guogu Chekeng Exhibition Hall. In 2000, a special museum, the Guoguo Museum, was built on the ruins of the Guoguo cemetery excavated for the second time.
